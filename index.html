About Ahmed Zewail

Ahmed Hassan Zewail (February 26, 1946 ‚Äì August 2, 2016) was an Egyptian-American chemist, known as the "father of femtochemistry".

He was awarded the 1999 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on femtochemistry and became the first Egyptian and Arab to win a Nobel Prize in a scientific field, and the first African to win a Nobel Prize in Chemistry.

Zewail was a professor of chemistry and physics at the California Institute of Technology (Caltech), where he was the first Caltech faculty member to be named the Linus Pauling Chair of Chemical Physics and served as the director of the Physical Biology Center for Ultrafast Science and Technology.

Femtochemistry

Zewail pioneered **Femtochemistry**, the study of chemical reactions on the fundamental timescale of atomic motion: the **femtosecond** ($10^{-15}$ seconds). His innovative use of ultrafast lasers allowed scientists to capture a **"molecular movie,"** observing the fleeting **transition state**‚Äîthe exact moment chemical bonds break and form. This work revolutionized physical chemistry and earned him the 1999 Nobel Prize.

VISUAL: Electron pulse used to probe structural changes in a material lattice.

Ultrafast Electron Diffraction (UED)

Expanding his methods, Zewail significantly advanced **Ultrafast Electron Diffraction**. This technique uses **short electron pulses** instead of light to study dynamics. It provides unmatched resolution, coupling the **femtosecond time scale** with **atomic-scale spatial resolution**, crucial for visualizing the structural dynamics in gas-phase molecules and materials in real-time.

4D Electron Microscopy (4D UEM)

Zewail successfully applied his ultrafast principles to biological systems, developing **Four-Dimensional Electron Microscopy** (4D UEM). This breakthrough adds the critical dimension of **time** to the high-resolution electron microscope image. It enables scientists to visualize the structure and dynamics of complex biological molecules and nanoscale materials, such as the "nano-drumming" motion in graphite sheets.

Enduring Legacy

Zewail City of Science and Technology

Zewail founded and donated his entire Nobel Prize money to establish this research institution in Egypt. It was his vision to create a world-class scientific research hub in the Middle East.

Awards in His Honor

The American Chemical Society established the **Ahmed Zewail Award for Ultrafast Science and Technology**, and Chemical Physics Letters established the **Ahmed Zewail Prize in Molecular Sciences**.

Educational Impact

Zewail inspired **generations of scientists** in Egypt and across the Arab world, showing that scientific excellence knows no geographical or cultural boundaries.

Science Diplomacy

As a **US Science Envoy to the Muslim world**, Zewail worked to bridge cultures through science and education, fostering international collaboration.
